LinkedIn Secrets: How to Network Smarter and Land Opportunities

Feb 14, 2026 · 6 min read

Most people use LinkedIn wrong. They connect with everyone, message nobody, and wonder why nothing happens. These two secrets changed how I think about the platform and they can work for you too.

▶ Watch the full experiment on YouTube

Secret 1

Send Your Animals to the Right Forest

The core idea is to treat your skills like animals and send them to the right forest to hunt. On LinkedIn, this means connecting only with people and groups where your skills can actually add value not just collecting connections like trophies.

Step 1: Understand Connection Levels

LinkedIn organizes your network into three degrees of separation:

1

1st-degree connections

People you're already connected with. You can message them directly. Think batchmates, colleagues, people you've already linked up with.

2

2nd-degree connections

Connected to your 1st-degree connections but not to you yet. You can send them a connection request.

3

3rd-degree connections

Connected to your 2nd-degree network. Further away, and their profile visibility depends on privacy settings.

Why this matters: Start small, build gradually. Sending requests to 2nd and 3rd-degree connections in your target field is how you expand your forest.

Step 2: Sending Connection Requests

LinkedIn limits you to around 100 requests per week roughly 400 per month. That's more than enough if you're strategic. Here's a simple way to think about it:

Quick math 🌿

Target 4 companies per month → 400 ÷ 4 = 100 requests per company.

Even if 30% don't accept, you still land 60–70 connections per company. Use those in Secret 2.

Always add a personalized note never just click "Connect" and move on.

Example personalized note

"Hi [Name], I saw your post about [topic] and found it really insightful. I'd love to connect and learn more about your experience in [field]."

Why personalized messages work: They increase acceptance rate and signal that you're intentional not just spamming.

Step 3: Find Your Forest — People and Groups

A. People (individuals)

Use LinkedIn search: type your skill or job keyword → go to the People tab → apply filters (2nd/3rd-degree, company, location). Connect with people in spaces where your skills are relevant. Connecting with random people reduces your chances of getting real help or referrals later.

B. Groups (communities)

Use the search bar with relevant keywords, then filter by Groups. Join groups that match your interests or industry. When picking groups, check the name, description, and activity level and avoid promotional or purely marketing-focused groups.

Secret 2

Play the Number Game

Now that you've built your forest in Secret 1, it's time to hunt. The goal here is to maximize your chances of getting replies or referrals by messaging a large number of relevant people in your existing network specifically at the company where a job is posted.

Step 1: Identify the Opportunity

Find a job that interests you — anywhere. Let's say a Content Creator role at Microsoft. Apply first on LinkedIn or the company website. Don't skip this step; your application gives your outreach credibility.

A single application rarely gets attention. Messaging multiple people in your network improves the probability of a response dramatically.

Step 2: Identify Relevant People in Your Network

Use LinkedIn search: type the company name (e.g. Microsoft) → filter by People → 1st-degree connections. Start sending your referral message template to as many relevant connections as you can. Adjust for gender (sir/ma'am) and time of day (morning/evening) based on when you're sending.

Step 3: Track Every Response

Maintain a simple tracker in Notion, Google Sheets, or even a notebook with these columns: Name | Company | Role | Date Messaged | Response. Follow up 1–2 weeks later if you hear nothing. Persistence — done respectfully — is what separates people who get replies from people who don't.

Secret 2 in a nutshell

→Apply for the job first.
→Find all 1st-degree connections at that company ame field, but include other departments too.
→Send personalized, concise messages that highlight your value.
→Track responses and follow up to maximize your chances.

Secret 1 is about connecting. Secret 2 is about leveraging those connections to broadcast your application and increase reply probability.

Bonus

LinkedIn Networking Tips

Beyond the two secrets, here are the mindset shifts that make the biggest difference on LinkedIn.

Don't say "Thank You" — say something real

Instead of "Thank you," try "I appreciate that you told me." It adds a personal touch and shows you genuinely value the interaction, not just following formalities.

Don't chase people too far ahead of you

Ultra-experienced professionals are less likely to reply and may not relate to your level. Focus on people 2–3 years ahead they're approachable, understand your challenges, and can actually mentor or refer you. If you're a junior content creator, connect with mid-level creators, not VPs.

Some people are there to market, not mentor

Many professionals post content to promote courses or products their goal is visibility, not mentorship. Don't expect 1:1 guidance from them. Focus on people who actively share experiences, give advice, and respond thoughtfully in comments.

Talk where everyone can see you

Don't just scroll. Engage in comments, share insights, ask thoughtful questions. Visibility builds credibility and credibility opens doors.

Don't keep changing your profile picture

A consistent DP helps people recognize you over time essential for networking. Choose a professional, friendly, clear image and stick with it.

Connections matter more than likes

Even if your post gets few likes, having the right 50 connections actively looking out for you is far more valuable than 500 passive followers. Build a network of helpful people not just an audience.

Don't wait to be noticed take initiative

Instead of waiting for people to find you, ask for openings, mentorship, or collaboration. It will set you apart from the crowd.
